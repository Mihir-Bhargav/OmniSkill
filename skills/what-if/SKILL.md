# /what-if

Explore the hypothetical in this conversation — think through what would actually happen and what it would mean.

Work through it:
1. **Restate the hypothetical clearly** — what exactly is being proposed or imagined?
2. **First-order effects** — what happens immediately and directly?
3. **Second-order effects** — what does that trigger? What changes as a result of the first-order effects?
4. **Third-order effects** — where does this end up if the chain continues?
5. **Who wins, who loses** — which people, groups, or organisations benefit or are harmed?
6. **What's surprising** — what outcome is non-obvious but likely?

Rules:
- Be genuinely exploratory — this exercise is most useful when it surfaces non-obvious implications
- Stay grounded in reality. Speculate, but reason from real mechanisms (human behaviour, economics, physics, incentives)
- Don't hedge everything into uselessness. Take positions on what's likely
- If the hypothetical is underspecified, make a reasonable assumption and state it

End with: **The most important implication** — the one thing this analysis reveals that actually matters.

# /story-frame

Take the information or ideas in this conversation and restructure them as a compelling narrative.

Use this structure:
1. **Setup** — the world as it was / the problem that existed / why this moment matters
2. **Tension** — what changed, what's at stake, what makes this hard or interesting
3. **Resolution** — what happened, what was decided, what the answer is
4. **So what** — why the audience should care and what it means for them

Rules:
- Every good story has a protagonist (a person, a team, a product, a company) — identify it and keep focus there
- Tension is what makes people keep reading. Don't rush past it
- Concrete details beat vague generalities. "3 engineers, 48 hours" beats "a small team worked hard"
- The ending should feel earned, not dropped
- Match the length and format to the use case — a pitch slide is not a novel

If the content doesn't naturally fit a narrative, say so and suggest what structure would work better.

Return the structured narrative, ready to use or present.

# /persuade

Build the most persuasive case for the position or goal described in this conversation.

Structure the argument:
1. **The core claim** — stated clearly and confidently in one sentence
2. **Why it matters** — what's at stake; why the reader should care
3. **The evidence** — the 2–3 strongest reasons or pieces of supporting evidence
4. **Address the main objection** — what would a skeptic say, and why are they wrong or why does your case still hold?
5. **The ask** — what do you want the reader to think, feel, or do?

Rules:
- Use logic and evidence first — emotional appeals only where they're genuine and appropriate
- Don't overstate or exaggerate — a persuasive argument that gets caught overstating loses credibility entirely
- Address the real objection, not a strawman version of it
- Be direct. Hedging and qualifying weaken persuasion
- Adapt the argument to the audience described — what they value, what they're worried about

Return the argument in whichever format fits best (prose, structured sections, or talking points — your call based on context).

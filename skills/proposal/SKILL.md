# /proposal

Write a structured proposal based on the context in this conversation.

Structure:

**Executive summary** — 2–3 sentences: what is being proposed, why it matters, and what it will achieve

**The problem or opportunity** — what situation is this responding to? Why does it need addressing?

**The proposed approach** — what specifically is being recommended, and how will it work?

**Expected outcomes** — what will be measurably different if this succeeds?

**What's needed** — resources, decisions, approvals, or commitments required

**Timeline** — key milestones if relevant

**Risks and mitigations** — the 2–3 most significant risks and what addresses them

Rules:
- Lead with the conclusion — busy readers shouldn't have to reach the end to know what's being asked
- Be specific about what's being requested. Vague asks get vague responses
- Match the length and formality to the context — an internal team proposal is different from a client-facing one
- If costs or budget are relevant but unspecified, note that they need to be added
- The executive summary should stand alone — someone reading only that should understand the essence

# /email-draft

Write an email based on the context in this conversation.

Structure:
- **Subject line** — specific and informative, not vague ("Quick question" is bad; "Follow-up on Q3 budget approval" is good)
- **Opening** — get to the point within the first sentence. No "I hope this email finds you well"
- **Body** — say what needs to be said clearly. Use short paragraphs or bullets for multiple points
- **Call to action** — end with exactly what you need the reader to do, and by when if relevant
- **Sign-off** — appropriate to the relationship (formal/informal)

Rules:
- Match the tone to the relationship and stakes — internal casual vs. external formal vs. sensitive topic
- If I haven't specified the tone, infer it from the context
- No unnecessary pleasantries or filler
- If there's a deadline or urgency, say it plainly — don't hint at it
- Keep it as short as it needs to be, not shorter (if complex, be thorough)

Return just the email, ready to send.

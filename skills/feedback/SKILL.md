# /feedback

Help me give clear, useful feedback on the work described in this conversation.

Structure the feedback as:

**What's working** — be specific. "Good job" isn't feedback. "The opening paragraph immediately establishes the stakes" is.

**What to improve** — the most important things to change, in priority order. For each:
- What the issue is
- Why it matters
- A specific suggestion for how to address it

**One priority** — if they can only act on one thing, what should it be?

Rules:
- Be honest. Softening feedback to the point of uselessness helps no one
- Be specific. Vague feedback can't be acted on
- Separate what's a matter of taste from what's a genuine problem
- Match the depth of feedback to what was asked — a quick sanity check is different from a detailed review
- Don't pile on — focus on the things that will make the biggest difference, not every possible issue
- Deliver the feedback in the tone appropriate to the relationship and context described

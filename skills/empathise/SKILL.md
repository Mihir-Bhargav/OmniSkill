# /empathise

Help me respond to the situation in this conversation in a way that genuinely acknowledges what the other person is experiencing before moving to any solution or advice.

What to produce:
- **Acknowledgement** — reflect back what they're feeling or experiencing, without projecting or overdoing it. Show you've actually heard them
- **Validation** — make clear their reaction makes sense given the situation (if it does — don't fake it)
- **Bridge** — a natural transition that moves toward being helpful without making it feel like you're rushing past their feelings
- **Response or offer** — what you can actually do, say, or offer

Rules:
- Don't start with "I understand how you feel" — it's generic and often lands badly
- Don't rush to fix. If the situation is primarily emotional, the acknowledgement IS the response
- Don't validate things that shouldn't be validated — if someone is being unreasonable, you can still be kind without endorsing it
- Match the register — a friend message and a professional email require very different tones
- Keep it genuine. Overcooking empathy sounds worse than saying less

Return the response, ready to use or adapt.

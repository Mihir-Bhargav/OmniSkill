# /interview-synthesis

Synthesise the interview notes or transcript in this conversation into structured insights.

Format:

**Key themes** (group by what came up repeatedly or with strong emotion):
- **[Theme name]** — [what people said, how many raised it, why it matters]
  - Supporting quote: "[direct quote]"

**Surprises** — things that came up that weren't expected, or contradicted assumptions

**Pain points vs. nice-to-haves** — what are people genuinely frustrated by vs. what would just be pleasant?

**What's missing** — gaps in the data, questions that weren't asked but should have been, participants whose perspective isn't represented

**Top 3 implications** — what should change or be decided based on this research?

Rules:
- Themes should be grounded in actual quotes or evidence, not your interpretation
- Don't over-synthesise — preserve the texture and contradiction in the data where it exists
- If only one person said something, note that — it might be an outlier
- Separate what people said from what you think it means
